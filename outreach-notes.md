# Outreach Notes

> Where to find contributors and advisors, and how to talk about the project honestly. Keep the framing accurate: independent, prospective, unpaid, not NASA-affiliated.

## Where to look

- University aerospace, mechanical, ECE, and robotics departments (clubs, labs, capstone teams)
- Space Studies / space industry programs and alumni groups
- Maker / fabrication communities (CNC, welding, 3D printing)
- Robotics meetups and online communities
- LinkedIn (space systems, payload integration, ISAM)
- Relevant subreddits and Discords for spaceflight and engineering

## Short pitch (copy/paste)

> I'm organizing an independent team to put together a Phase 1 application for NASA TechLeap's Robotically Manipulated Payload Challenge. The concept is a compact robotic servicing validation payload that demonstrates repeatable manipulation tasks in low Earth orbit. It's unpaid, prospective, and not NASA-affiliated — but it's a credible, well-documented engineering project either way. Looking for technical contributors and advisors. Interest form: https://forms.gle/FzjG6L8gPRDjCkvA9

## What to be clear about every time

- This is an **independent, prospective** effort — not an official NASA/TechLeap/Luminary Labs page.
- It is **unpaid/volunteer**; prize funding is not guaranteed.
- Contributors must meet the **official challenge eligibility requirements**.
- Any award compensation would follow a **written team agreement** set before submission.

## What NOT to collect

Do not ask anyone for sensitive personal information (citizenship status, immigration details, date of birth, home address, government ID). Direct people to the official rules to confirm their own eligibility.

## Follow-up workflow

1. Review interest form responses.
2. Group by skill area and availability.
3. Reach out to strong fits for a short intro call.
4. Confirm they've reviewed the official rules and eligibility.
5. Slot into roles (see `team-roles.md`) and share the concept brief.
